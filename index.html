TOP ATTRACTIONS

Spike Island

In the last 1300 years Spike Island has been a home to many and a host to a 6th century Monastery, Fortress, and the largest convict depot in the world in centuries of island homes. Today, visitors can discover the islands 104 acres and Take the scenic ferry ride from Kennedy Pier in Cobh, enjoy a guided tour of the beautiful views, admire the stonework of the fort island or relax in our cafe and picnic areas.Spike Island is the winner of Europe’s leading tourist attraction 2017 at the World travel Awards!

Blarney Castle and Gardens

Historic Blarney Castle is famous for its stone, which according to legend; the stone has the power to give anyone who kisses it the ‘gift of the gab’. Inside the castle, visitors can climb the battlement to kiss the stone, take in the views, and explore the stunning stone building. Outside, visitors can discover the castles grounds to find the gardens and Rock Close.

English Market

The English Market is municipal food market in the center of Cork and has been a market on the present site since 1788. It has variety of fresh produce from around the world and is best known for fresh fish, butchers, and serving local specialties such as spiced beef and drisheen. Queen Elizabeth II visited the market in 2011 and in 2016 and 2017; the English Market was used as a location for the film The Young Offenders and TV series..
